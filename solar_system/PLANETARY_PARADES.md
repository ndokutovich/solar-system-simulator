# Planetary Parades - Quick Reference

## What is a Planetary Parade?

A "planetary parade" or "planetary alignment" occurs when multiple planets appear close together in the sky as seen from Earth. **Important**: Planets are never perfectly aligned in a straight line - they just appear in the same region of the sky from our perspective.

## Why Aren't They Perfectly Aligned?

Planets orbit at different:
- **Speeds**: Inner planets move faster
- **Inclinations**: Orbits tilted at different angles
- **Eccentricities**: Elliptical, not circular orbits

**Result**: Planets never form a perfect line, but sometimes cluster in the same general direction from Earth.

## Fun Facts

### Rarity of Alignments

- **3-planet alignment**: Several times per year
- **4-planet alignment**: A few times per decade
- **5-planet alignment**: Every 20-30 years
- **All 8 planets visible**: Very rare (June 2022 was special!)
- **Perfect alignment** (all in a line): Never happens (and wouldn't cause anything special if it did)
